Overview

The Wallet & Transaction module is designed to manage reseller financial operations with real-time balance tracking, monthly analytics, credit control, and payment reconciliation.

The system must clearly differentiate between:

Recharged Balance (System Credit)
Actual Payments Received
Unpaid Liabilities
Credit Limit Utilization
Security Deposits
Dashboard – Stats Cards Requirements
1. Current Wallet Balance

Displays the real-time usable wallet balance of the reseller.

Logic:

Reflects the current available balance after all recharges and deductions
If unpaid liabilities exist, system may optionally show:
“Adjusted Balance” or “Net Position”
2. Current Month Recharge

Shows the total amount of balance credited (recharged) to the reseller within the current month, regardless of payment status.

Includes:

Paid recharges
Credit-based recharges
3. Current Month Paid Payment

Displays the total actual payments received from the reseller during the current month.

Important:

Only includes confirmed incoming payments
Not linked to recharge amount directly
4. All Unpaid Balance

Represents the outstanding amount where:

Reseller has received balance (recharge)
BUT has not yet made the payment

System must provide:

Total unpaid amount
Monthly breakdown
Transaction-level details
5. Credit Limit

Displays the approved credit limit assigned to the reseller.

Logic:

Defines how much unpaid balance is allowed
Any recharge beyond paid amount increases unpaid liability
Must enforce:
Cannot exceed defined credit limit (optional rule)
6. Security Deposit

Shows the initial deposit collected from reseller at onboarding.

Purpose:

Acts as financial security
Can optionally be adjusted against unpaid balances
Recharge Flow – Functional Logic
Step 1: Recharge Initiation

Admin enters:

Recharge Amount
Payment Received (optional)
Reseller चयन
Step 2: System Behavior
Case A: Recharge Without Payment (Credit Mode)
Recharge added to wallet
Same amount added to Unpaid Balance
Case B: Recharge With Exact Payment

Example:

Recharge = 5,000
Payment Received = 5,000

Result:

Wallet Balance ↑
Paid Amount ↑
No unpaid balance
Case C: Overpayment Scenario (Important Case)

Example:

Recharge = 2,000
Payment Received = 10,000

System Logic:

2,000 → applied to recharge
Remaining 8,000 → automatically adjusted against Unpaid Balance

If unpaid exists:

Deduct from unpaid
Move that amount to Paid Payments

If no unpaid exists:

Store as:
Advance Payment OR
Wallet top-up (based on system rule)
Step 3: Payment Reconciliation

When payment is received:

System should automatically:
Reduce unpaid balance
Increase paid amount
Maintain transaction logs
Important Balance Logic (Core Concept)

System must maintain three parallel financial states:

1. Wallet Balance
Usable system credit
2. Unpaid Balance
Liability (what reseller owes)
3. Paid Amount
Actual cash flow received
Recharge Preview (UI Requirement)

When admin clicks “Add Recharge”, system should display:

Current Wallet Balance
Current Unpaid Balance
Credit Limit Remaining
After Recharge Balance
Payment Adjustment Preview

Separation of Recharge vs Payment
Automatic Payment Adjustment
Transparent Financial Tracking
Credit Control Enforcement
Audit-Friendly Ledger System
